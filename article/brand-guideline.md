# Brand Guideline

## Follow Popular Brand Guidelines

Study examples from trusted brands to understand standard structures and key elements like logo usage, color palette, typography, and tone of voice.&#x20;

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/Screen Shot 2025-01-05 at 10.34.24.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

## Use Templates for Fast Design

Choose pre-designed templates from tools like Figma to speed up the creation process while ensuring a professional look. Customize them to fit your brand’s identity.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/Screen Shot 2025-01-05 at 10.37.08.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

## Document the Style and Components

Clearly organize sections for each brand element: logo, colors, fonts, imagery, and more. Include examples and rules for proper and improper usage to maintain consistency across all applications.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-12-09 at 16.41.45 (2).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/Screenshot 2024-12-11 at 10.01.40.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

## Finalize

Review the guideline for completeness and accuracy. Export it as a PDF for easy sharing and keep an editable version for future updates. Distribute to all relevant teams for consistent implementation.
