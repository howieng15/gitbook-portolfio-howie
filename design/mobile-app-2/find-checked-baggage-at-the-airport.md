# Find checked Baggage at the Airport

## Document

### 1. Project Overview

This project addresses the passivity passengers face when searching for checked baggage at airports. The goal is to design a combined hardware (tracker) and software (mobile app) system that allows users to proactively track and retrieve their luggage efficiently.

* **Role**: UI/UX Designer
* **Process**: Understand -> Research -> Analyze -> Iterate -> Design

### 2. The Problem

After long flights, passengers often experience high stress and frustration due to:

* **Extended Wait Times**: Waiting at baggage carousels can sometimes exceed one hour.
* **Uncertainty**: A lack of real-time information leads to "baggage anxiety" and a sense of helplessness.
* **Risk of Loss**: Fear of luggage being lost, misplaced, or mistakenly taken by others.
* **Communication** **Barriers**: Difficulty in accessing immediate information or help from airport staff.

### 3. Research & Market Insights

* **Market Statistics**: In 2022, approximately 26 million bags were lost globally.
* **User Feedback**: Interviewed passengers expressed a desire to be "more proactive" and noted that current methods (like airport announcements) are unreliable.

### 4. Analysis

#### User Persona

* **Demographics**: All ages (10-100), no limits on gender, occupation, or income.
* **Goal**: Receive checked baggage as soon as possible.
* **Frustrations**: Long wait times and the persistent risk of luggage loss.

#### Customer Journey Map

The typical journey involves waiting, potential worry when the bag doesn't appear, and a tedious support process if the bag is lost. Opportunities were identified in using a tracking device to provide visibility, allowing users to contact support early if their bag is confirmed to be out of range.5. The SolutionDeveloping a GPS Tracker integrated with a mobile app featuring:

* Device Management: Easily add devices via QR Code or activation code.
* Real-time Tracking: Access a live map to see exactly where the luggage is located.
* Smart Notifications: Alerts are sent when the bag is within a 3m radius (nearby) or outside a 30m radius (potentially lost/out of range).

### 6. Design & Iteration

#### Interaction Flow

1. Preparation: The user attaches the tracker to their luggage at home.
2. Arrival: Upon landing, the user opens the app to monitor the bag's location on the map.
3. Retrieval: The app sends a notification as the bag approaches the carousel, ensuring a smooth pickup.

#### Business Feasibility

* Revenue Streams: Profits primarily from selling tracking devices, but can also include paid downloads, subscriptions, or in-app purchases.
* Estimated Budget: Development and launch costs are estimated between $70,000 and over $300,000, covering UI/UX, hardware manufacturing, and marketing.

### 7. Conclusion

Research confirms that a tracking app and device are feasible and highly desired by users. To succeed, the product must focus on the European market (where baggage issues are high) and offer a user experience that surpasses existing competitors like Bag iTag.<br>
